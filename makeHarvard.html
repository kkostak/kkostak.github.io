Scaled Input Mirroring Robot

Design Process

This project was initially made during the MakeHarvard 24-hour makeathon in Febuary 2025. We were inspired by an input mirroring robot that our Electronics and Controls professor, Felix Huang, showed in class. Our spin on this robot was to have the output side scale down the mirrored motion. After MakeHarvard, we decided to apply to the 2025 MassRobotics Form & Function Challenge happening at the end of April with this prototype, and we got accepted! I'm super excited to iterate on our robot and implement some of the ideas that were much too lofty to do in 24 hours! But first lets look at how our robot currently works

Mechanical System

On the mecanical side of things, we designed the mechanism using Fusion360. We really wanted to utilize capstan drives in order to move the linkages, especially the further away one (or "elbow" link as we called it) as using strings instead of gears would reduce the weight of the linkages, resulting in less deflection. This was also the reason we decided to put both motor mounts at the base of the linkages (or at the "shoulder"). During the hackathon, we had lofty goals of printing the linkages out of carbon fiber to further reduce the weight, but after multiple failed prints we decided to stick with PLA for the sake of time, which still ended up working pretty well!

On the electrical side of things, we used rotary encoders on the input side, stepper motors on the output side, and a Raspberry Pi to send the information over. These design choices were largely made based on what was available to us during the makeathon, however we are currently looking at getting better hardware now that we are competing in the Form & Function Challenge! This project is still very much in project so stay tuned for updates!
