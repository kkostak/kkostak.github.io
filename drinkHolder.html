Assistive Wheelchair Drink Holder

Client

This was a group project done for ME20: Engineering Design 1 consisting of me and tw. For this project, we were given a local Somerville client that had Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy, which made it so that they were unable to move below the neck. Becuase of this, they have trouble drinking without the help of a caretaker to raise the drink up to thier mouth. Currently, they have a stationary drink holder that is essentially just an elevated table that allows them to drive their powered wheelchair up to the cup and drink, however, this solution is extremely limited in that it does not solve this issue when they are outside of the house. Our task was to now create a device that would enable our client to independantly drink on the go. .

One solution that was initially considerered was to utilize a goose neck drink holder in order to utilize its flexibilty, however after talking to our client, the flexibility that the goose neck offered also meant that it would drift out of place over time, thus rendering the drink inaccessable to our client. We also considered using a long tube to bring the liquid in the drink closer to his face, however this would alter the taste and therefore wasn't a realistic option.
