PCB Design

This was a project done for Tufts' ME30: Electronics and Controls, and was also my first exploration into custom PCB manufacturing! Our first task was to build a power supply PCB that could take in 12V and output 5V and 3.3 V. I first designed a circut on a breadboard to make sure I understood where things needed to go, and then used this as a blueprint on how to design & lay out the PCB. I used the same design process for the H-Bridge, which essentially enables the direction of a circut to switch (which is really useful for making a motor spin two different directions!).
